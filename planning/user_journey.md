## How It Works Together: User Journey

**Day 1 – Setup**
- Elderly patient (or family member) visits site. Enters age, weight, allergies,illness,conditions.
- Adds current medications: takes photos of existing pill bottles. App builds active medication list and schedules.

**Day 3 – New Prescription**
- Patient visits clinic, receives handwritten prescription for a new antibiotic.
- Opens app, takes photo. App extracts drug name and dose.
- App checks: dose is correct for weight. **Drug-drug interaction detected:** new antibiotic interacts with blood pressure medication patient already takes.
- App displays: *"This new drug may affect your blood pressure medicine. Show this screen to your pharmacist."*
- Patient shows pharmacist, who adjusts dosing timing to avoid interaction.

**Day 5 – Adherence**
- 8:00 AM reminder: *"Time to take your blood pressure medicine."*
- Patient takes pill, taps "Taken." App logs it.
- 2:00 PM, patient wonders: *"Did I take my morning dose?"* Opens app, home screen shows: *"Last taken: Today, 8:03 AM."*
- No double-dose occurs.

**Day 30 – Clinic Follow-Up**
- Patient sees a new doctor. Doctor asks what medications they're on and whether they've been taking them.
- Patient opens app, taps "Share History." Shows doctor the medication list and adherence calendar.
- Doctor sees patient has missed 3 doses of blood pressure medication in the past month. Adjusts treatment plan accordingly.
